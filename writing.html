Poems

Ignorance is not a Virtue

I must say
That I strongly dislike you
You say that you will be supportive of me
But all I have seen so far
Are meaningless condescending words

You must learn
That you are not always right
You may have decades of experience
Yet you seem to have never changed

You must learn
That however well-disguised it is
Ignorance
Is not a virtue
For Just because you are authority
Does not mean you are better than me

You yell at me nearly daily
But have you ever taken a breath
Set the rusty gears in motion
And allow reasoning to prevail
Over your cruel emotions?

For why should I
Treat you with respect
When you have never done
The same for me?

Ten Minutes to Midnight


Tonight, long after the cold sun dipped below the horizon
And the stars circle above
In a small study-room I sit
A worn birch table before me
Topped with a neat stack of paper.

I grasp a black gel-pen
Pick up a sheet with my thumb and forefinger
Narrow my eyes
Scan over the black printed letters
Swallow a cup of green tea
And begin to write -
Hands moving rapidly over the pages.

At long last I finish
Twenty-nine pieces of paper
Now with the black scribbles of words
Scattered all over the floor,
Stained with varying amounts of spilled tea.

A glance at the ninety-cent clock -
Ten minutes to midnight.

Blurred Lines


The snow still lingers
When the first birdsong
Breaks the eerie silence.

The air is still crisp
When the blinding sun
Shines its rays on the earth.

The heat of summer still beats
When the leaves begin to show
Their inner color.

The squirrels still scutter
When the first snow
Falls onto the landscape.
